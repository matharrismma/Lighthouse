The Almanac · the ledger of falsifiable claims

The keeping, three views.

Kept every entry · with verdict Signed entries with operator-signed receipts Flagged where readers disagree

Every entry is a claim with a verdict. CONFIRMED — the engine has tested it and it holds. MIXED — true under conditions, false under others; the conditions matter. MISMATCH — popularly believed, but the engine has shown it wrong. OBSOLETE — was once correct; reality moved on. Carry what survives. Discard what doesn't.

In the lineage of Foxfire — Appalachian folk knowledge verified by use. Foxfire documents what survives the test of doing; the Almanac documents what survives the test of computing. Each claim carries the reasoning the engine used to reach its verdict, so you can check the engine's work yourself.

Suggest an entry

Hand the engine an old saying or a multi-domain situation. It runs polymathic and returns a draft entry — verdict + verification + suggested triggers — for the curator to review. Drafts are not auto-published; the curator (Matt) decides what earns a place in the book.
