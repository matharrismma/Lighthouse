The book

The Almanac

A curated record of what the engine has worked through. Two kinds of entry — protocols, canonical multi-domain situations pre-run through the verifiers, and sayings, old proverbs and rules of thumb confirmed (or, in some cases, quietly retired) by computation. Each entry has the math behind it and a short note on what the math actually shows. The Almanac does not panic.

In the lineage of Foxfire — community-gathered Appalachian folk knowledge, plainspoken, verified by use. Foxfire documents what survives the test of doing. The Almanac documents what survives the test of computing. Same posture, different verifier.

Suggest an entry

Hand the engine an old saying or a multi-domain situation. It runs polymathic and returns a draft entry — verdict + verification + suggested triggers — for the curator to review. Drafts are not auto-published; the curator (Matt) decides what earns a place in the book.
