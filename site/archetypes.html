Archetypes.

Three witnesses fix a point on a line. A life is a curve. Archetypes are the shape of the curve — the way a kind of person tends to meet a kind of moment. The engine surfaces the closest patterns; the reader names whether they fit. Scripture is the primary substrate. Literature and history sit below as secondary patterns — same schema, lower authority.
