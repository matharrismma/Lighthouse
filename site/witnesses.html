Why this page exists

The third of the four gates is BROTHERS — the two-witness rule from Deuteronomy 19:15. The engine refuses to admit a claim on a single voice. That refusal needs a body. This page is that body.

“One witness shall not rise up against a man for any iniquity, or for any sin, in any sin that he sinneth: at the mouth of two witnesses, or at the mouth of three witnesses, shall the matter be established.” Deuteronomy 19:15

What follows is the public roll of named persons who have agreed to stand as witnesses for discernments produced by the engine. Each entry is signed with the witness’s own Ed25519 public key. Bearing false witness on the roll is grounds for removal — recorded openly and dated. (We are following Deut 19:16–21 as a principle, not enforcing its sanctions; the proportionate consequence available to us is removal from the roll.)

What this is not. Being on the Witness Roll does not make anyone a biblical witness in the Old Testament legal sense, a priest, an elder, or any other office. It is a software role with a clear scope: read the matter, sign or decline, stand behind your name. Anyone reading more into it than that is reading it wrong.

What a witness does

  • Stands under a real name. Display name on the roll matches a verified person. No pseudonyms.
  • Holds their own key. Each witness controls their Ed25519 private key locally. The site holds only the public key. Compromise of a key requires re-issuance and an entry on the roll.
  • Signs discernments brought to them. A discernment that passes RED and FLOOR is sent to two or three witnesses on the roll. They read the package and sign or decline. The signature is recorded on the permanent /d/<slug> page; the decline is logged privately to the operator.
  • Bears the named consequence. A witness who is found to have signed in bad faith (corruption, malice, or culpable negligence) is removed from the roll, with the date and the reason published. Deuteronomy 19:16–21 is the principle we take after — the penalties prescribed there were for Israel’s courts; here removal from the roll is the proportionate consequence available to us.
  • Is not a moderator or an editor. The witness does not curate the site, does not vote on which discernments appear, does not receive a salary, does not rank users. The witness reads the matter brought to them and signs or declines.

How to join the roll

Four steps. No fast track.

  1. Submit an application below (or email witness@narrowhighway.com if you prefer). Your real name, a way to reach you, and a few lines on who you are. The operator reads every application.
  2. Interview with the operator. A short conversation — not a job interview, an honesty interview. The questions take after Deuteronomy 19: do you understand what you would be standing for? Can you decline cleanly when you would not sign? The operator runs this conversation in the practical sense; we do not claim it as an ecclesial office.
  3. Generate a key. Locally. The operator will share a short guide; common wallets (Coinbase, Rainbow, Frame) and standalone Ed25519 tools both work. The site receives only the public half.
  4. First signature. Once on the roll, a low-stakes discernment is sent first — a familiar test of the signature flow. After it clears, the witness is at full standing.

There is no fee to join the roll, and no payment for serving on it. The witness’s portion, if any payment is involved at all, is the same statutory portion any witness in a recovered Covenant Testimony matter receives under federal law — paid out of recovery, by Act of Congress, never by the platform. What this refuses

Pseudonymous witness. A signature without a name is not a witness. The roll requires a real display name and a verified identity held by the operator (not displayed publicly beyond the display name itself).

Bot-witnessing. No automated agent will ever appear on the roll. The BROTHERS gate is human-to-human by design.

Witness-for-pay. No one is paid to sign. The statutory portion in a Covenant Testimony matter is paid out of recovery, by Act of Congress, to the witness in that matter. Not a salary, not a retainer, not paid by us.

Inherited standing. Being well-known elsewhere does not put you on the roll. The interview is the same for everyone.

Silent removal. A witness removed from the roll is removed in the open, with a date and a one-sentence reason, signed by the operator.

“A faithful witness will not lie: but a false witness will utter lies.” Proverbs 14:5

For technical readers — the signature flow

Each signature on a discernment is a detached Ed25519 signature over a canonical JSON serialization of the discernment package. The public key, the signature, and the timestamp are stored alongside the discernment record and displayed on /d/<slug>. Verification is local and deterministic; no server-trust is required to verify a signature against the witness’s published public key.

Witness pubkeys served from GET /witness/roll.json. Per-witness attestations from GET /witness/by/<pubkey>. (Endpoints under construction.)
