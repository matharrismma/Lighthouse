The Connection Graph

Verified connections, and candidate resonances — kept strictly apart. The trail is the trust.

The engine does not assert connections it cannot back. Three tiers, kept strictly apart:

Verified cross-domain — one claim, independently confirmed true by two or more deterministic domain verifiers at once (e.g. penicillin across biology, chemistry, and medicine). Each carries its computation trail. This is the moat: verified, not generated, and cross-domain.

Verified scripture — sources bound by a shared witnessed verse: two or more that both cite it are connected through it, and that citation passed the witness gate (Deut 19:15).

Candidate resonances — cross-domain edges whose claims merely share conceptual axes. Suggestive, not verified — a starting point for inquiry, never a claim of truth. Labeled as such, kept last.
