Privacy

The Concordance Engine (narrowhighway.com) is a verification service: you submit a derivation or computational claim, and it checks the claim against fixed external standards and returns a verdict. It does not require an account and does not ask for personal information.

What you submit

The verification endpoints accept the claim itself -- for example, a mathematical expression to check. Do not submit personal, confidential, or sensitive information as part of a claim; the service is for computational and logical statements, not personal data.

Seals are permanent and public

If you ask the engine to seal a result (seal: true), it mints a permanent, content-addressed receipt at https://narrowhighway.com/seal/<hash>. That receipt is public and intended to be permanent so that anyone can independently re-check the verdict. Only seal claims you are willing to publish. If you do not set seal: true, no receipt is created.

What we do not do

The service does not sell your submissions and does not use them to build advertising profiles. It is a conduit that verifies and records; it does not generate answers or act as an authority.

Operational data

Requests are logged for reliability, security, and abuse prevention, and the logging is privacy-preserving: the service records a truncated IP prefix (never your full IP address), a coarse user-agent class, the request path, and the referring page — used only in aggregate for traffic analysis and rate-limiting. It does not require an account, does not request personal information, and uses no third-party advertising trackers.
