# HORIZONS — one year, ten years, and what to build now

Companion to [THE_GUIDE.md](THE_GUIDE.md). The map gives the rungs; this gives
the timeline and the backcast.

**The discipline:** build now only what is expensive to change later; defer
everything whose cost is flat. Foundations whose cost grows with every file and
every family get laid today, while they are cheap. Features whose cost is the
same whenever you add them wait until demand asks for them.

## One year out (mid-2027)

- Channel 1 live and stable — the FAST channel resilient (the RTMP-cap failure
  solved by the supervisor) — and **Lighthouse Kids** launched behind it.
- The Guide front door works: ask anything → narrow → a floor-true answer with
  the trail. The narrowing is multi-level (rung 2), not one-shot.
- The gap-ledger has been running for months, so coverage has grown from 65
  verifiers by following real gaps, not guesses. The floor reaches further
  every week with almost no new code.
- QB stages 2–3: first sponsor-as-studio deals, the ~1000-true-fans base
  forming, the ~$200/mo baseline covered. No ad spend, ever.
- The Codex compiling continuously and signed; SEO bringing real human
  retrieval visible in the granular traffic split; the distribution loop
  running — every piece a quiet commercial pointing home.

## Ten years out (2036)

- The borrowed mouth is nearly gone. Deterministic walls cover most of everyday
  life's questions; the Guide answers coherently on almost no power; the LLM is
  a fading scaffold used only at the frontier.
- The Guide is a trusted front page for Christian families — "the good old days
  in a box" with a modern engine underneath. A real alternative starting page,
  not a novelty.
- A network, not a channel — auteur + cable-access + human-certified + kids +
  per-region FAST; the home-economics platform / marketplace mature.
- The Codex is a canonical, signed, citable manuscript — a decade of attested
  provenance behind it.
- The witness stands: ten years of fruit, families demonstrably helped, the
  arrows landed on Christ — and the floor open enough that other agents can
  stand on it. Self-replicating cells, the current paying for the future; many
  small buildings, not one big one.

## What to build now — the load-bearing foundations

Cheap today at a handful of tools; ruinous later at forty tools and 100,000
families. These are the only things that must be built ahead of demand.

1. **Contracts, not implementations.** Freeze and *version* the floor's I/O —
   `stand_on_floor()`, the card/packet schema, the gate-verdict shape — and make
   every room conform. **The** bet: if every tool talks to the floor through one
   stable contract, the engine can be swapped underneath (shrink the mouth, rung
   3) in year five without touching a single room. If the contracts drift, the
   reach needs a full rebuild.
2. **Run the gap-ledger from today.** Every query the floor can't fully answer,
   logged with enough structure to become a verifier spec. You cannot
   retroactively log the year you didn't.
3. **Sign from the first artifact.** Ed25519 provenance on packets and the Codex
   *now*. A decade of attestation is only possible if you start at year zero.
4. **Own the substrate; never rent the moat.** Keep acquiring PD you *own*; keep
   storage discipline. Anything licensed or scrapable can be pulled out from
   under you; the owned library + generated substrate is the durable asset.
5. **Get the household / identity / trust / feedback model right now.** Who a
   family is, what they trust, where they are on the journey. Retrofitting
   identity onto anonymous data is a rebuild.
6. **One funnel, one front door.** Every new capability is a *lens on the one
   engine*, never a new silo. This keeps the 10-year surface coherent instead of
   eighty stranded pages.
7. **Keep the distribution-as-content loop running.** Watermark, outro,
   deep-link home — organic, no ad spend. Audience compounds like the ledger;
   it only compounds if it starts now.

## The hedge worth naming

Every bet above rests on the **floor** and treats the model as a swappable
**borrowed mouth**. That makes the project *antifragile to the AI landscape*,
which over ten years is genuinely unknowable. If models get cheaper, banned,
commoditized, or superhuman — it doesn't matter to us, because the floor, the
owned substrate, the signed provenance, and the schema don't depend on the
mouth. It is the one design that wins in every branch of the next decade,
because the house is never bet on the model. That is architecture, not luck.

## What to NOT build now (cost is flat — wait for demand)

The token economy (Phase 4+), the crypto rail (Phase 5), a GPU purchase
(revenue-funded later), the full marketplace aggregator (it hits the
operator-load ceiling), and most of the channels (build them when the audience
signals). All cheap to add *whenever* — so building them early only steals time
from the foundations above.

*Don't Panic.*
