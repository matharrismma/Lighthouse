About

Narrow Highway is a curated internet for Christian families. The good ole days in a box.

What it is

One safe site that holds the things a family needs in one place — entertainment, study, tools, music, prayer, a way to ask hard questions, a way to play. Built so a child who has only this site has enough.

Underneath the retro surface is a real engine: a substrate of Scripture and patristics, alignment gates that filter every piece of content, signed receipts on what we keep, and 65 domain verifiers the engine consults when a claim touches their subject (physics, biology, theology, scripture-anchors, statistics, and the rest). The surface is the 70s-90s family-television feel. The engine is what makes it trustworthy.

Who serves here

The site serves Jesus Christ. It is a conduit, not a source. Good fruit is the measure.

You'll see Shepherd, our conversational layer, on every page (the chip in the bottom-right). Shepherd helps with navigation, schoolwork, tools, content. Shepherd is also willing — when asked — to speak plainly about Christ. Default mode is quiet servant. Once you ask the big questions, the answer is honest.

What we don't do

No advertising. No data collection beyond what serves you. No paywalls on the content. No begging. No tracking pixels. No engagement-bait. No content that's already widely available unless we add something — usually a pastoral observation at the end.

The economic model is the opposite of the modern web. Most of the content here is public domain. The marquee productions we make — animated shows, audio dramas — are sponsor-funded or free. If we ever charge for anything, it'll be for craft tier work (signed receipts, dedicated archives, custom production) — never for access to the content.

Who built it

Built by Matt Harris, with a tiny family team, with help from Claude and other tools that don't replace judgment — they extend it. Every piece of content passes an alignment gate before it ships. Every system that serves you is signed.
